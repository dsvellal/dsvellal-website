Now or Later??

August 5, 2007

This story is from Panchatantra, a book written by Vishnu Sharma. It has many stories which revolve around the five tantras — Mitrabhed, Mitrasamprapti, Sandhi, Vigraham and Apareekshitkarakam — the five techniques used in political science.

A farmer buys a piece of land for cultivation. The land, left uncultivated for many years, had become shelter for many animals and birds. All the animals had this gift — they could understand the human language. Among them, a wise owl made its shelter on one of the tall trees.

When the animals come to know that a farmer had bought this land, they go to the owl to ask for counsel. The owl says, "Don't panic. Let us not take any hasty decisions right now. We will wait for his next move. I want you guys to spy on him and tell me what he is talking about."

Day 1: The farmer tells his son, "Let us take the help of the villagers to get the trees removed." A squirrel reports this to the owl. The owl replies, "Don't worry. We don't have to think about moving for a while now."

A week passes. Nothing happens.

Day 8: The disappointed farmer says, "It was foolish of us to trust the villagers. Let us approach our relatives." A crow reports this. The owl again replies, "Don't worry, nothing is going to happen!"

Another week passes. Nothing happens.

Day 15: The dejected father tells his son, "It was foolish to trust our relatives. WE shall start early tomorrow and do our unfinished business." A rat reports this to the owl.

The owl summons all the animals and says, "My dear fellow animals, it's time that we pack our bags and leave this home to find another one."

Everyone is perplexed. A rabbit asks, "But why? All this time you were saying we need not worry. But now, when he alone is planning to do it, why do you fear?"

The owl replies:

"No one is ready to do your work, right? All this time, the farmer was depending on his fellow villagers or his relatives. But now, he has realized the flaw and he is going to start his work tomorrow, without anyone's help. So it's better we start packing!"

Moral: Self help is the best help :)
