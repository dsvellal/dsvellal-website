An Atheist v/s a Believer

April 29, 2009

I have been listening to lectures on Mahabharata and Bhagavad Gita by Dr. K S Narayanacharya. This morning, a strange thought came to me.

Point 1: A Believer believes in an external entity (GOD). An Atheist does not — he believes in himself.

Point 2: A Believer says he derives energy from an infinite source. An Atheist says he derives energy from within — he IS the energy.

Point 3 — and this is the beautiful one:

A Believer says "everything I do is an inspiration from HIM — I surrender my actions and results to Him." An Atheist says "I am self-inspired."

This third point reveals two different paths for spiritual elevation:

  • For a Believer — the path of Dwaita philosophy (selflessness, surrendering to a higher consciousness)
  • For an Atheist — the path of Advaita philosophy (self-realization)

Ultimately everyone attains a higher state of mind — the Believer with awareness of the path, the Atheist without. And agnostic people? They can achieve it through Karma Yoga.

I am a believer. At this moment, I still believe in Dwaita, but hopefully one day I will understand and realize Advaita. Nevertheless, I am happy traveling any path that leads to my enhancement — spiritual or otherwise :)
