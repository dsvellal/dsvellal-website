Finally, She Spreads Her Wings...

February 3, 2009

I have asked folks what they would do with an interesting book. The reply is always the same — finish it as quickly as possible. The eagerness to see how the plot ends dominates the subtle details. I am also a victim of curiosity. But of late, I have found joy in savoring a book.

Today, I finished "Gently Falls the Bakula" by Sudha Murthy. Thanks to my friends Srikanth and Harini for introducing me to this book. The story revolves around Shrikanth, an intelligent and ambitious man, and his equally intelligent yet introverted wife Shrimathi, who always went out of her way to help her husband achieve success.

Points that impressed me:

  1. The writing style and the Indian History woven into the narrative
  2. The emotions — Shrimathi's struggles and how she handled things. At one point, when she was in a dilemma, I was literally shouting "No, no, what the hell are you doing!" 😄
  3. An important lesson: Value of an object is known only when it is lost
  4. The title — Bakula (Maulsari), a flower known for its fragrance even when it dries out
  5. The commitment and sacrifice both had to make
  6. How Shrimathi was able to break out of self-constructed boundaries to pursue her happiness

For books I enjoy, I always find that: page numbers flip very fast, chapters are covered rapidly, and time flies. This book has refreshed my memory on the little things that made — and still make — me happy :)
