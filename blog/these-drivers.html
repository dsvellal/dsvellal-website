Puh!! These Drivers..

February 17, 2009

Note: All characters in this story are fictional. This is my first venture into fiction.

On his way back from office, Treyada was listening to his favourite song from his iPod. He had cleverly concealed it — clipping it to his pants pocket, running the wire under his shirt, ear buds hidden under his helmet. A perfect blend.

"Why drive, when you can sleep," was what Treyada used to say about preferring a 90-minute bus ride. But circumstances had forced him to drive. He had two companions — his first girlfriend, Kinetic Nova (6 years and counting), and his iPod.

Traffic fascinated him. Six months of 24km daily commute had made him a smart driver. "Breaks — what a wonderful way to use friction! Apply friction when you want to slow down, otherwise keep the accelerator at full throttle. That is the best way to drive, my friend."

Something caught his eye — an ambulance. Its siren was piercing his melodious music. "Puh!! These drivers, they do not know how to drive smart! That guy could have easily taken the service road."

He wanted to race the ambulance. Raising the accelerator, feeling the engine's vibration. "Ah, my sweet baby. From 0-60 in just 8 seconds, they said, but you can do much more, can't you?" He weaved through traffic.

"Just a few more meters!" The excitement grew as he saw the ambulance's red light flashing. A Scorpio was the only obstacle. No space on the right — the only way was a sharp left. "What the heck, let's do it buddy!" He leaned left, right wrist made a swift move—

CRAAASSSSHHHHHH !!! Everything went blank.

When he opened his eyes, everything was white. His mother was sitting beside him, a tear silently slipping from her eyes. "You've been unconscious for 3 days. There was an uncovered manhole — you drove right into it."

His mother was talking about how carelessly youngsters drive, about traffic rules, about something more. All these did not make sense to him. He was tired, very tired. The last thought that entered his mind was—

"Puh!! These drivers....."
