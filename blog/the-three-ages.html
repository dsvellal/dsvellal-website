The Three "Ages" of My Life

February 11, 2009

My life has three ages:

Physical age — the age of my body. Lack of physical activity quickens the aging process.

Action age — the type of action I take in a situation decides my "action age." It's how we judge people as "mature" or "immature."

Thought age — what influences me to take an action. One major contributor: my thoughts at that moment. The older my thought age, the more maturity it brings to my actions.

Consider two scenarios with a cockroach — in the first, I kill it out of disgust. In the second, I realize it's a living creature with equal right to live, and let it crawl away in peace. Same stimulus, different thought ages.

An elder at Yogashree posed this dilemma:

A train is approaching on Track 1 where 9 kids are playing (knowing it's dangerous but choosing it for shade). 1 cautious kid plays alone on Track 2. You are the station master with a lever. What do you do?

Her answer was profound: "The perspective you see is limited. In life, there is always a bigger perspective — a higher consciousness — that we do not see." She compared it to a mother calling her child inside before rain — the child sees only restriction, the mother sees care and foresight.

"Even though things seem not to go our way, if we think from a state of higher consciousness, the actions taken are the best among our choices. At this elevated state, we forgo our benefits and think about others."

The thought-action cycle: your thoughts affect your actions, which in turn affect your thoughts. Once the process of cleansing is initiated, it's a cyclic chain — one influences the other automatically.

A Kannada saying captures it: "Drushti badalaayisu, Drushya badalaagutte" — Change the way you look at things, and see how beautifully the scenery changes.
