The Alchemist — Deciphered!

December 28, 2009

While on a 3-day off to Mysore for Dasara, I discovered a fascinating parallel between Paulo Coelho's famous quote from The Alchemist and the teachings from Indian yoga philosophy.

"When you want something, all the universe conspires in helping you to achieve it."

This statement talks about the External Universe conspiring in your favor. Beautiful? Yes. But there's more.

In my pursuit of understanding yoga philosophy, I came across a deeper concept: Sankalpa Yoga.

Sankalpa means resolve, intention, or what's often misinterpreted as "stubbornness." But according to Yoga Sutras, it's deeper. It's when your resolve is so strong and so pure that it changes your internal physiology.

You see, the Internal Universe has its own elements:

  • Thoughts and desires (mental energy)
  • Emotions (emotional energy)
  • Vital energy (pranic energy)
  • Physical body (material energy)

When your Sankalpa is aligned and pure, all these internal elements reorganize themselves to support it. Your mental clarity sharpens, your emotional state stabilizes, your vital energy channels become more efficient, and your physical body responds accordingly.

So I deciphered Coelho's statement like this:

"When you want something with a pure and strong Sankalpa, your entire Internal Universe conspires to help you achieve it."

The External Universe conspiring? That's Coelho. The Internal Universe conspiring? That's Yoga. Both are beautiful. One completes the other.

These thoughts came to me in the Mysore palace, looking at a painting of the Wadiyar dynasty. Sometimes the best revelations come from the stillest moments :)
