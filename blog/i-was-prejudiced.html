I Was Prejudiced...

December 20, 2009

December 18, 2009 — Avatar movie released in 3D. Booked the 1pm show at PVR Gold Class. Expectations were through the roof: "3D, James Cameron, and Gold Class!"

After the initial hype, I realized December 19 was one of the worst days in a long time. Why? Because my expectations were so high that I failed to enjoy the actual experience. I was so focused on "how good it should be" that I didn't appreciate "how good it already was."

Rewind: a few months earlier, I rode one of those banana boat rides in Goa — with zero expectations. Result? I had the time of my life! The cheap, simple ride was infinitely more enjoyable than the gold-class 3D extravaganza.

The difference? Prejudice. For Avatar, I was full of it. For the banana ride, I had none.

Lesson learned:

Go see anything with the intention of enjoying the thing. Not with specific expectations. The moment you start expecting, your prejudice takes over. And then, experience suffers.

PS: I am a sincere James Cameron fan and Avatar is a wonderful visual experience. But that day, my prejudice got the better of me.
