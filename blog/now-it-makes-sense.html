...Now It Makes Sense!

January 20, 2010

During my first year of engineering, I wrote a poem called "A Ray of Hope". It was an assignment for English class, inspired by the theme of how small acts of kindness can illuminate lives. The poem won some appreciation, but honestly, back then I didn't fully grasp the depth of what I had written.

Years later, re-reading it, everything clicked.

Give a little, take nothing
Smile a while, cry a little
For the world is round and what goes around comes around
Be a candle, burn yourself to light others' way
And in that burning, find your brightest day

When I wrote those lines at 18, they were just words strung together for an assignment. At 25, they became my philosophy.

The essence: giving is not about generosity — it's about understanding your role in the ecosystem. A candle doesn't "sacrifice" when it burns; it fulfills its purpose. Similarly, when we give, we're not being noble — we're simply being useful.

There's a beautiful concept in the Upanishads — that divinity is not external but participatory. We are part of the divine system. And our participation — through giving, through kindness, through selfless action — is what keeps the system alive.

What I couldn't understand at 18, life taught me by 25. And now, rereading my own words... it finally makes sense.
