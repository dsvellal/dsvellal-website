My Teachers :)

December 14, 2008

An incident from two years back, during my final year of engineering. I was at a bhel puri shop with my friend Pavan when we saw a middle-aged couple with a 3-4 year old girl. The kid had a bouncing ball — those dense rubber balls with vivid colour patterns in the core. She was very happy playing with it.

Then disaster struck — the ball slipped from her hand and started rolling towards the road. The kid looked at her mother with a mixed expression of confusion, sadness, and tension. Then came the expression I can never forget — the way she extended her hands and wriggled her fingers towards the rolling ball, as though asking it to magically return, simultaneously looking at her mother with an almost-crying face.

My actions were out of instinct — I ran after the ball, stopped it, and returned it to the kid. She gave me the sweetest of smiles, holding her mother's pallu and hiding half her face behind it. By god, that was the best smile a girl has given to me ever till date :)

Looking back, this small incident taught me so many things:

  1. Value beyond money: To her, that 15-rupee ball meant a whole world of happiness. Money is not always the means that brings happiness.
  2. Deep involvement: Her level of involvement in playing was something we "civilized beings" have lost.
  3. Expression beyond words: Sometimes an action can make more sense than 1000 words.
  4. Contentment: She was very happy with her new toy, even though it did not cost a penny!
  5. Innocence: Everything in its infancy looks good to my eyes — I believe it's because of the innocence.
  6. The SMILE: My actions didn't cost me much, but the return was far more than expected. This is what Krishna says in the Bhagavad Gita — "KarmaNye vaadhikaarasthe maa phaleshu kadaachana" — do your work and leave the results to me.

There are two sorts of happiness in this world — happiness that comes with a reason, and happiness that comes without a reason. The former can be taken away if the reason is taken away. But the latter stays, because you don't need a reason to be happy. You are JUST HAPPY :) — and that is exactly "Why God is laughing!"
