Influence

January 15, 2008

Long ago, there lived a king. He was wise and just. His kingdom was prosperous. One day, the king had a dream — a wolf dancing in front of his throne. Curious to know the meaning, he summoned his best scholars but none could give a satisfactory answer. So he made an announcement: the person who would give a satisfactory answer would receive 1000 gold coins.

A woodcutter heard the announcement. While cutting a tree, a bird spoke to him, "I know the meaning of the dream. What will I get in return?" The woodcutter promised half the reward, and the bird revealed the meaning.

The woodcutter told the king: "The wolf symbolizes treachery. Your dream indicates betrayal in the kingdom." The king was pleased, gave the reward, and deployed spies. The guilty were punished. But the woodcutter never shared with the bird — instead buying a new house.

Months later, the king dreamt of a sword above his palace. Again the woodcutter sought the bird's help, who forgave the first offense. The meaning: enemies preparing to attack. The king rewarded 2000 gold coins and won the subsequent war. But the woodcutter, instead of paying the bird, threw a stone at it.

Finally, the king dreamt of white lambs in the royal court. The bird, feeling pity, again told the meaning: peace and prosperity restored. The king gave 3000 gold coins. This time, the woodcutter returned with gratitude to repay everything.

The bird replied:

"Oh foolish woodcutter, if I were after money, I could have told the king directly. The first time, there was treachery in the kingdom, and the environment forced you to cheat me. The second time, there was violence, and the environment forced you to hurt me. But the third time, when justice was restored, you came to your senses."
"Common man generally gets influenced by the environment he lives in. It's not his fault if he acts accordingly. Few people are able to overcome this urge, and their actions always reflect truth irrespective of the environment. They, my friend, are true YOGIS."

Whenever people talk about peer pressure or about actions they are forced to take, I always remember this story. It's hard to deviate from the normal course, but inside us there is a YOGI, waiting to be awakened.
